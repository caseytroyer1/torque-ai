Drill Library

Practice these drills to improve specific areas of your game

Setup Beginner

Wall Posture Drill

Builds spine angle awareness and corrects slouching at address.

  • 1Stand with your heels, hips, and upper back lightly touching a wall.
  • 2Hinge forward from your hips until your hands hang below your shoulders — your backside should leave the wall slightly.
  • 3Hold for 5 seconds, memorize the feeling, then step away and replicate it without the wall.
Feel
Tall spine, weight in the balls of your feet, arms hanging freely.
Setup Beginner

Alignment Stick Drill

Grooves consistent alignment of feet, hips, and shoulders at address.

  • 1Place one alignment stick on the ground pointing at your target along your toe line.
  • 2Place a second stick parallel, just inside the ball position.
  • 3Take your address and check that feet, knees, hips, and shoulders are all parallel to the sticks.
Feel
Square and balanced — like you're standing on a train track aimed at the target.
Setup Beginner

Knee Flex Check

Fixes locked or over-bent knees that cause balance issues throughout the swing.

  • 1Stand straight, then slightly unlock your knees — just enough that they aren't rigid.
  • 2Hinge from the hips into your address position maintaining that soft knee flex.
  • 3Bounce lightly on your feet — if you feel balanced and springy, your flex is correct.
Feel
Athletic and ready — like a shortstop waiting for a ground ball.
Setup Beginner

Shoulder Level Drill

Corrects uneven shoulders that cause mis-hits and inconsistent ball flight.

  • 1Hold a club across your chest with both hands and take your address.
  • 2Look in a mirror or have someone check that the club shaft is level or your trail shoulder is only very slightly lower.
  • 3For irons, shoulders should be nearly level. For driver, trail shoulder drops slightly due to ball position.
Feel
Level and relaxed across the shoulders — no tension or tilt.
Backswing Beginner

Feet Together Drill

Improves balance and weight transfer by forcing you to stay centered.

  • 1Place your feet together touching, grip down on a short iron.
  • 2Make slow half swings focusing on staying balanced throughout.
  • 3Gradually build to full swings — if you fall over, your weight transfer is off.
Feel
Quiet lower body, rotation happening around a stable center.
Backswing Beginner

Left Arm Straight Drill

Trains a wide swing arc by keeping the lead arm extended through the backswing.

  • 1Take your grip and place your lead arm straight out in front of you at address.
  • 2Make a slow backswing focusing on keeping that arm from bending past 10-15 degrees.
  • 3At the top, your lead arm should feel taut and connected to your chest rotation.
Feel
A wide, sweeping arc — like you're wrapping your arms around a large barrel.
Backswing Intermediate

Hip Coil Drill

Builds hip-shoulder separation and X-Factor for more power without swinging harder.

  • 1Cross your arms over your chest and take your address position.
  • 2Rotate your shoulders fully as if making a backswing while resisting with your hips — feel the tension build in your core.
  • 3Repeat 10 times to groove the feeling of separation before hitting balls.
Feel
A coiled spring in your torso — shoulders turned, hips resisting.
Backswing Intermediate

Step Drill

Exaggerates weight shift and hip drive to build proper downswing sequencing.

  • 1Start with your feet together at address.
  • 2As you start your backswing, step your lead foot out to normal width.
  • 3Let the step trigger your downswing — hips lead, then arms, then club.
Feel
Lower body initiating the downswing — like throwing a ball with your whole body.
Impact Beginner

Spine Angle Hold Drill

Eliminates early extension — one of the most common causes of thin and fat shots.

  • 1Take your address and place a club across your shoulders behind your back.
  • 2Make slow practice swings focusing on keeping your spine angle constant from address through impact.
  • 3If the club points down at the ball at impact, your spine angle is maintained correctly.
Feel
Your chest staying down and through — like you're covering the ball at impact.
Impact Intermediate

Hip Bump Drill

Trains proper hip clearance and sequencing through the impact zone.

  • 1From the top of your backswing, pause for one second.
  • 2Start the downswing by bumping your lead hip toward the target — before your arms move.
  • 3Let the hips clear and rotate fully as your arms drop naturally into the slot.
Feel
Hips leading, arms following — like cracking a whip from the ground up.
Impact Intermediate

Gate Drill

Fixes an over-the-top path and eliminates the pull and slice.

  • 1Place two tees just wider than your clubhead on either side of the ball.
  • 2Make slow swings focused on swinging through the gate without hitting either tee.
  • 3A correct path exits the gate to the right of the target (for right-handers).
Feel
Club approaching from inside — swinging out to right field through the ball.
Impact Intermediate

Head Behind Ball Drill

Stops the head from sliding forward which causes thin shots and loss of power.

  • 1At address, place a headcover or towel just outside your lead foot.
  • 2Make swings ensuring your head stays behind the ball position through impact.
  • 3Your head should only move forward after the ball is gone — during follow through.
Feel
Head staying back while hips drive forward — creating a reverse C at impact.
Tempo Beginner

Pause at the Top

The single best drill for golfers who rush the downswing.

  • 1Make your full backswing and deliberately pause for 2 full seconds at the top.
  • 2From the pause, start the downswing slowly — let gravity begin the movement.
  • 3Do 20 reps without a ball before hitting. Your tempo will feel dramatically smoother.
Feel
The club feeling heavy at the top — patience before the release.
Tempo Beginner

Count Rhythm Drill

Builds a repeatable 3:1 backswing-to-downswing ratio used by tour pros.

  • 1Count out loud: "one-one-thousand, two-one-thousand, three" on your backswing.
  • 2Say "hit" as you strike the ball — the downswing should take about 1 count.
  • 3Practice this counting rhythm for 10 minutes — it resets rushing faster than any other drill.
Feel
Backswing feels almost too slow — downswing happens automatically and effortlessly.
Short Game Beginner

Clock Chipping

Builds precise distance control around the greens.

  • 1Imagine a clock face around the ball. Swing your lead arm to 7 o'clock (short), 8 o'clock (medium), 9 o'clock (full).
  • 2Hit 5 chips to each position and note where each distance lands.
  • 3On the course, pick your clock position based on the distance needed.
Feel
Pendulum motion — same tempo every time, only the length changes.
Short Game Beginner

One Hand Putting

Exposes weaknesses in your putting stroke and builds feel in both hands.

  • 1Putt 10 balls using only your lead hand — focus on keeping the face square.
  • 2Putt 10 balls using only your trail hand — feel the difference in control.
  • 3Now putt with both hands — it should feel noticeably smoother and more connected.
Feel
Both hands working as one unit — no dominant hand taking over.
Short Game Intermediate

Towel Under Arms Drill

Connects your arms to your body for more consistent chip and pitch shots.

  • 1Tuck a small towel under both armpits and grip the club normally.
  • 2Make chip and pitch swings — if the towel falls, your arms are disconnecting from your body.
  • 3Keep the towel in place for 20 consecutive shots before removing it.
Feel
Arms and chest moving together as one connected unit through the shot.
